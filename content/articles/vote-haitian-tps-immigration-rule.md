The easiest way to describe the House vote is also the least sufficient one. A Republican-led House broke with Donald Trump and voted to protect Haitian immigrants. That is true. The House passed a bipartisan bill 224 to 204 to restore Temporary Protected Status for Haitians for three years, with 11 Republicans joining Democrats after the Trump administration moved to strip those protections. The bill now moves into an uncertain Senate and toward the possibility of a veto. That happened. It is still not the real account. To understand what the vote actually revealed, it helps to separate the visible event from the structure beneath it. The visible event looked like a rare act of moderation. The structure beneath it was dependence. The mechanism that forced that dependence into view was pressure.
